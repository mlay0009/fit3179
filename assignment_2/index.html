Sydney Primary Schools

Education in Sydney

The Sydney primary school education system consists of three main categorical sector types which are Government, Independent, and Catholic. Government schools receive public fundings from the NSW Department of Education, prioritising quality education to all students. These schools are known for their dedication in accessibility, ensuring alignment with educational standards, regardless of student socioeconomic status.

Independent type shcools operate independently of government control, allowing to create own curriculum and teaching methods. Most Independent Schools are associated with religious or cultural groups, offering diverse educational opportunities. Catholic schools, which emphasize moral and spiritual development in addition to academics, are the final major sector in Australian primary education. Students in Catholic schools benefit from a holistic education that incorporates faith into their learning experience, and they frequently foster a strong feeling of community.

As observed from the analysed data, majority of primary schools in Sydney are government-run and are evenly located throughout the city. On the other hand, most Independent schools tend to have higher diversity, shown by the size of each circles.



Students Attendance Rate

In 2018, NSW government schools established national guidelines for reporting student attendance statistics. Due to the inclusion of part-day absences and accounting for student mobility in the calculation, most schools' attendance rates fell. As a result, data from 2018 onwards could not be compared with previous years.

In the year 2020, data on student attendance during Semester 1 showed a significant decline. This downfall can be attributed to the unprecedented circumstances brought about by the global pandemic, COVID-19. During this time, educational institutions across the globe were faced with the challenge of adapting to a new way of teaching and learning, which, in many instances, included remote and online education.

Sydney Primary School ICSEA Score



In Australian primary schools, the ICSEA score, which stands for the Index of Community Socio-Educational Advantage, is a measure used to assess the socio-economic background of a school's student population. ICSEA scores are calculated based on the level of education, employment, and income of student parents, offering useful insights into the diversity and socioeconomic status of students.
